Problem 13 — 1.4 Exercises (Determining Continuity)

Let \( k(x)= \begin{cases} 6 - x &x \leq p \\[2ex] x^2 + 4 &x \gt p. \end{cases} \)

Find the values of \(p\) for which \(k(x)\) is continuous at \(x = p\).

SOLUTION In order for \(k(x)\) to be continuous at \(x = p\), \(\lim_{x \to p} k(x)\) must equal \(k(p)\).

We see that \(k(x) = 6 - x\) for \(x \leq p\). Because \(x = p\) is included in the left-hand-side definition of \(k(x)\), we know that \(\lim_{x \to p^-} k(x) = k(p) = 6 - p\). Moreover, the right-hand-side limit, \(\lim_{x \to p^+} k(x)\), must also be equal to \(6 - p\). Equating these expressions and solving for \(p\) produce \[ \begin{aligned} \lim_{x \to p^-} k(x) &= \lim_{x \to p^+} k(x) \\ 6 - p &= p^2 + 4 \\ p^2 + p - 2 &= 0, \\ \end{aligned} \] for which the solutions are \(p = -2\) and \(p = 1\). Therefore, for \(p = -2\) and \(p = 1\), \(k(x)\) is continuous at \(x = p\).
