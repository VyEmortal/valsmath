Problem 2 — 1.4 Exercises (Determining Continuity)

Consider Fig. 1.4-5., in which the graph of \(g(x)\) is shown. Determine whether \(g(x)\) is continuous at \(x = -1\), \(x = 0\), and \(x = 2\).

SOLUTION Because \(g(-1)\) is not defined, \(g(x)\) is not continuous at \(x = -1\).

At \(x = 0\), \(g(0)\) is defined, and we notice that \(\lim_{x \to 0} g(x) = g(0)\). Therefore, \(g(x)\) is continuous at \(x = 0\), which is intuitive because we could trace the graph of \(g\) without lifting our pen off the paper.

We notice that, at \(x = 3\), \(g(x)\) has a jump discontinuity. Moreover, the only other discontinuity in the graph is at \(x = -1\). Therefore, for \(-1 \lt x \lt 3\), \(g(x)\) is continuous. This interval includes \(x = 2\), so we conclude that \(g(x)\) is continuous at \(x = 2\). Again, at \(x = 2\), we could trace the graph of \(g(x)\) without lifting our pen off the paper.
