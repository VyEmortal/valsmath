Problem 15 — 1.4 Exercises (Determining Continuity)

Let \( f(x)= \begin{cases} px + q &x \leq 2 \\[2ex] px^2 - 2q &x \gt 2. \end{cases} \) It is known that \(f(x)\) is continuous at \(x = 2\) and \(f(1) = 4\). What are the values of \(p\) and \(q\) ?

SOLUTION Because \(f(x)\) is continuous at \(x = 2\), we can establish that \(\lim_{x \to 2} g(x) = g(2)\). We therefore have, after equating the one-sided limits, \[ \begin{aligned} p(2) + q &= p(2)^2 - 2q \\ 2p + q &= 4p - 2q \\ 2p &= 3q. \end{aligned} \]

Additionally, because \(f(1) = 4\), we can establish that \[ \begin{aligned} p(1) + q &= 4 \\ p + q &= 4. \end{aligned} \]

We now have the following system of two equations, which permits us to solve for the two unknown quantities \(p\) and \(q\). \[ \begin{aligned} p + q &= 4 \\ 2p - 3q &= 0. \end{aligned} \] It is easiest to rewrite \(p + q = 4\) as \(2p + 2q = 8\) because we can then subtract the equations. Doing so gives \[ \begin{aligned} 2p + 2q &= 8 \\ 2p - 3q &= 0 \\ 5q &= 8 \\ q &= 8/5. \\ p &= 12/5. \end{aligned} \] Therefore, given that \(f(x)\) is continuous at \(x = 2\) and \(f(1) = 4\), \(\displaystyle p = \frac{12}{5}\) and \(\displaystyle q = \frac{8}{5}\) satisfy these conditions.
