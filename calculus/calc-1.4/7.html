Problem 7 — 1.4 Exercises (Determining Continuity)

Let \( f(x)= \begin{cases} x^2 - 3x + 8 &x \lt -1 \\[2ex] \sin(x + 1) &x \geq -1. \end{cases} \)

Determine whether \(f(x)\) is continuous at \(x = -1\).

SOLUTION To establish continuity for \(f(x)\) at \(x = -1\), we must verify that \(\lim_{x \to -1} f(x) = f(-1)\). In this case, taking the one-sided limits, we obtain \[ \begin{aligned} \lim_{x \to -1^-} f(x) &=(-1)^2 - 3(-1) + 8 \\ &= 12. \\ \lim_{x \to -1^+} f(x) &= \sin(-1 + 1) \\ &= 0. \\ \end{aligned} \] Because the one-sided limits are not equivalent, \(\lim_{x \to -1} f(x)\) does not exist. Therefore, the function is discontinuous at \(x = -1\).
