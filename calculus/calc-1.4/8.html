Problem 8 — 1.4 Exercises (Determining Continuity)

Let \( v(x)= \begin{cases} \sqrt{1 - x} &x \leq 1 \\[2ex] \log_4(x) &x \gt 1. \end{cases} \)

Determine whether \(v(x)\) is continuous at \(x = 1\).

SOLUTION To establish continuity for \(v(x)\) at \(x = 1\), we will determine whether \(\lim_{x \to 1} v(x) = v(1) = 0\). In this case, considering the one-sided limits gives \[ \begin{aligned} \lim_{x \to 1^-} v(x) &= \sqrt{1 - 1} \\ &= 0. \\ \lim_{x \to 1^+} v(x) &= \log_4(1) \\ &= 0. \\ \end{aligned} \] Thus, because \(\lim_{x \to 1} v(x) = v(1) = 0\), we confirm that \(v(x)\) is continuous at \(x = 1\).
