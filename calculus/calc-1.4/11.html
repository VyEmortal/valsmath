Problem 11 — 1.4 Exercises (Determining Continuity)

Let \( g(x)= \begin{cases} k^2 - 4x &x \leq 2 \\[2ex] kx &x \gt 2. \end{cases} \)

Find the values of \(k\) for which \(g(x)\) is continuous at \(x = 2\).

SOLUTION We consider the case in which \(g(x)\) is continuous at \(x = 2\), from which we can solve for \(k\). We see that \(g(2) = k^2 - 8\). Since we assume that \(g(x)\) is continuous at \(x = 2\), we assume that \(\lim_{x \to 2} g(x)\) exists and equals \(g(2)\). we take the one-sided limits and set them equal to each other. Considering the left-hand-side limit, we have \[ \begin{aligned} \lim_{x \to 2^-} g(x) &= k^2 - 4(2) \\ &= k^2 - 8. \end{aligned} \] Taking the right-hand-side limit, we get \[ \begin{aligned} \lim_{x \to 2^+} g(x) &= (2)x \\ &= 2k. \end{aligned} \] Setting these limits equal to each other and solving for \(k\) give \[ \begin{aligned} k^2 - 8 &= 2k \\ k^2 - 2k -8 &= 0 \\ \end{aligned} \] \[ \therefore k = -2 \, \, \textrm{and} \, \, k = 4. \] Thus, \(k = -2\) and \(k = 4\) permit \(g(x)\) to be continuous at \(x = 2\).
