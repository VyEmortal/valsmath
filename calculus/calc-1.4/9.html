Problem 9 — 1.4 Exercises (Determining Continuity)

Let \( g(x)= \begin{cases} 3^{\sqrt{8 - x}} &x \leq 4 \\[2ex] x^2 - 3x + 4 &x \gt 4. \end{cases} \)

Determine whether \(g(x)\) is continuous at \(x = 4\).

SOLUTION To establish continuity for \(g(x)\) at \(x = 4\), we must confirm that \(\lim_{x \to 4} g(x) = g(4)\). We know that \[g(4) = 3^{\sqrt{8 - 4}} = 9.\] In this case, considering the one-sided limits gives \[ \begin{aligned} \lim_{x \to 4^-} g(x) &= 3^{\sqrt{8 - 4}} \\ &= 9. \\ \lim_{x \to 4^+} g(x) &= (4)^2 - 3(4) + 4 \\ &= 8. \\ \end{aligned} \] Because the one-sided limits are not equivalent, \(\lim_{x \to 4} g(x)\) does not exist. Therefore, \(g(x)\) is discontinuous at \(x = 4\).
