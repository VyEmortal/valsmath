1.4 Exercises — Determining Continuity

Click on a problem number to view the solution.


\(1.\)

Consider Fig. 1.4-4., in which the graph of \(f(x)\) is shown. Points \(A(-5, 4), B(-2.5, -5)\), and \(C(8, 3)\) are labeled, with point \(B\) being the location of a removable discontinuity. Determine whether \(f(x)\) is continuous at each point.


\(2.\)

Consider Fig. 1.4-5., in which the graph of \(g(x)\) is shown. Determine whether \(g(x)\) is continuous at \(x = -1\), \(x = 0\), and \(x = 2\).


\(3.\)

Determine whether \(3^{x + 4}\) is continuous at
\(x = 2\).


\(5.\)

Determine whether \(\log_3(x - 3)\) is continuous at \(x = 3\).


\(7.\)

Let \( f(x)= \begin{cases} x^2 - 3x + 8 &x \lt -1 \\[2ex] \sin(x + 1) &x \geq -1. \end{cases} \)

Determine whether \(f(x)\) is continuous at \(x = -1\).


\(9.\)

Let \( g(x)= \begin{cases} 3^{\sqrt{8 - x}} &x \leq 4 \\[2ex] x^2 - 3x + 4 &x \gt 4. \end{cases} \)

Determine whether \(g(x)\) is continuous at \(x = 4\).


\(11.\)

Let \( g(x)= \begin{cases} k^2 - 4x &x \leq 2 \\[2ex] kx &x \gt 2. \end{cases} \)

Find the values of \(k\) for which \(g(x)\) is continuous at \(x = 2\).


\(13.\)

Let \( k(x)= \begin{cases} 6 - x &x \leq p \\[2ex] x^2 + 4 &x \gt p. \end{cases} \)

Find the values of \(p\) for which \(k(x)\) is continuous at \(x = p\).

\(4.\)

Determine whether \(\sqrt{2 - 4x}\) is continuous at
\(x = 1/2\).


\(6.\)

Determine whether \(\sin^{-1}(x)\) is continuous at
\(x = 2\).


\(8.\)

Let \( v(x)= \begin{cases} \sqrt{1 - x} &x \leq 1 \\[2ex] \log_4(x) &x \gt 1. \end{cases} \)

Determine whether \(v(x)\) is continuous at \(x = 1\).


\(10.\)

Let \( h(x)= \begin{cases} \sin(x) &x \lt \pi/4 \\[2ex] \cos(x) &x \gt \pi/4. \end{cases} \)

Determine whether \(h(x)\) is continuous at \(x = \pi/4\).


\(12.\)

Let \( f(x)= \begin{cases} 8k - x^2 &x \lt 4 \\[2ex] k^2 \sqrt{x} - k^3 &x \geq 4. \end{cases} \)

Find the values of \(k\) for which \(f(x)\) is continuous at \(x = 4\).


\(14.\)

Let \( k(x)= \begin{cases} e^{2x} + 7 &x \leq q \\[2ex] 5e^x + 1 &x \gt q. \end{cases} \)

Find the values of \(q\) for which \(k(x)\) is continuous at \(x = q\).

.

\(15.\)

Let \( f(x)= \begin{cases} px + q &x \leq 2 \\[2ex] px^2 - 2q &x \gt 2. \end{cases} \) It is known that \(f(x)\) is continuous at \(x = 2\) and \(f(1) = 4\). What are the values of \(p\) and \(q\) ?
